DIABETES

Diabetes is a chronic (long-lasting) health condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. Your body breaks down most of the food you eat into sugar (glucose) and releases it into your bloodstream. When your blood sugar goes up, it signals your pancreas to release insulin.

CAUSES OF DIABETES: 

1.The exact cause of most types of diabetes is unknown. In all cases, sugar builds up in the bloodstream. This is because the pancreas doesn't produce enough insulin.
2.In most cases diabetes are caused due to the food items taken
3. Diabetes may be caused due to obesity also.

SYMPTOMS: 

1.Urinate (pee) a lot, often at night.
2.Are very thirsty.
3.Lose weight without trying.
4.Are very hungry.
5.Have blurry vision.
6.Have numb or tingling hands or feet.
7.Feel very tired.
8.Have very dry skin.

TYPES: 

There are three main types of diabetes: type 1, type 2, and gestational diabetes (diabetes while pregnant).
2. latent autoimmune Diabetes

PREVENTION: 

1.Type 2 diabetes is largely preventable by taking several simple steps: keeping weight under control, exercising more, eating a healthy diet, and not smoking.
2.Cut sugar and refined carbohydrates from your diet.
3.Making positive lifestyle changes such as eating a well-balanced diet, exercising regularly and getting down to a healthy weight (and maintaining it) are the key to possibly reversing or managing type 2 diabetes.
4.Lose extra weight. Losing weight reduces the risk of diabetes.
5.Be more physically active. There are many benefits to regular physical activity. 
6.Eat healthy plant foods. Plants provide vitamins, minerals and carbohydrates in your diet. 
7.Eat healthy fats. 
8.Skip fad diets and make healthier choices.
