KIDNEY AND LIVER PROBLEMS

Hepatorenal syndrome is a condition in which there is progressive kidney failure that occurs in a person with cirrhosis of the liver. It is a serious complication that can lead to death.
Hepatorenal syndrome (HRS) is a type of progressive kidney failure seen in people with severe liver damage, most often caused by cirrhosis.

CAUSES OF KIDNEY AND LIVER PROBLEMS: 

1.Heavy alcohol use.
2.Obesity.
3.Type 2 diabetes.
4.Tattoos or body piercings.
5.Injecting drugs using shared needles.
6.Blood transfusion before 1992.
7.Exposure to other people's blood and body fluids.
8.Unprotected sex.
9.Exposure to certain chemicals or toxins
10.Family history of liver disease

SYMPTOMS: 

1.Skin and eyes that appear yellowish (jaundice)
2.Abdominal pain and swelling.
3.Swelling in the legs and ankles.
4.Itchy skin.
5.Dark urine color.
6.Pale stool color.
7.Chronic fatigue.
8.Nausea or vomiting.
9.Loss of appetite.
10.Tendency to bruise easily.

PREVENTION: 

1.Monitor weight and eat a healthy diet.
2.A healthy diet that's low in sodium, processed meats, and other kidney-damaging foods may help reduce the risk of kidney damage.
3.Get active.
4.Quit smoking.
5.Getting a checkup?
6.Take medications as directed.
7.Keep your blood pressure below 140/90, or ask your doctor what the best blood pressure target is for you.
8.If you have diabetes, stay in your target blood sugar range as much as possible.
9.Exercise.
10.Wash produce and steer clear of toxins.
11.Prevent hepatitis A, B and C.
12.Watch out for medications and herbs.

DIET: 

The Items that will help you are:

1.Include vegetables and lean protein – like fish, eggs, poultry or legumes – at every meal, and limit your intake of saturated fat, sodium and sugar.
2.If you have an existing kidney or liver issue, talk to your doctor before starting any new meal plan.
3.The Healthy Kidney:
			Blueberries. Blueberries and other dark berries like strawberries and raspberries are among the best sources of antioxidants to help protect your kidneys.
			Cauliflower.
			Olive Oil.
			Garlic.
			Bell Peppers.
			Cabbage.
			Skinless Chicken.
			Arugula.
4.Good diet for liver problems:
			Eating fruits and vegetables and lean protein such as legumes, poultry, and fish.
			Avoid uncooked shellfish. 
			Taking vitamins and medicines prescribed by your health care provider for low blood count, nerve problems, or nutritional problems from liver disease. 
			Limiting your salt intake.
