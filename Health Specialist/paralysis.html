PARALYSIS

Paralysis is the loss of the ability to move some or all of your body. It can have lots of different causes, some of which can be serious. Depending on the cause, it may be temporary or permanent.

CAUSES OF PARALYSIS: 

1. Most Paralysis is due to strokes or injuries such as spinal cord injury or a broken neck.

2. Nerve Diseases such as amyotrophic laterial scierasis.

3. Autoimmune diseases such as GUILLIAN-BARRE SYNDROME.

SYMPTOMS: 

1. Inability to move part of your body, or not able to move at all.

2. Loss of sensation in arms and legs.

3. Reduced motor functions.

4. Loss in the ability to talk.

PREVENTION: 

1. Individuals must maintain a Balanced Diet.

2. Do Exercise for atleaset 30 minutes.

3. Must quit smoking.

4. Must opt for an annual health checkup to detect early paralysis symptoms.
