STRESS PROBLEMS

Stress that's left unchecked can contribute to many health problems, such as high blood pressure, heart disease, obesity and diabetes.

Stress is how we react when we feel under pressure or threatened. It usually happens when we are in a situation that we don't feel we can manage or control.
Sometimes, a small amount of stress can help us to complete tasks and feel more energised.
But stress can become a problem when it lasts for a long time or is very intense.
In some cases, stress can affect our physical and mental health.
1.Acute stress happens within a few minutes to a few hours.
2.Chronic stress lasts for a long period of time or keeps coming back.

CAUSES OF STRESS PROBLEMS: 

1.Feel under lots of pressure.
2.Face big changes in your life.
3.Are worried about something.
4.Don't have much or any control over the outcome of a situation.
5.Have responsibilities that you find overwhelming.
6.Don't have enough work, activities or change in your life.
7.Experience discrimination, hate or abuse.
8.Unhealthy lifestyle.
9.Taking on too much.

SYMPTOMS: 

1.Diffculty breathing.
2.Panic attacks.
3.Blurred eyesight or sore eyes.
4.Sleep problems.
5.Fatigue.
6.Muscle aches and headaches.
7.Chest pains and high blood pressure.
8.Indigestion or heartburn.
9.Irritable, angry, impatient or wound up
10.Over-burdened or overwhelmed
11.Anxious, nervous or afraid
12.Like your thoughts are racing and you can't switch off
13.Unable to enjoy yourself
14.Depressed
15.Uninterested in life

PREVENTION: 

1.Take care of yourself. Avoid drugs and alcohol as they can add to stress.
2.Engage in self-relaxation.
3.Take breaks when needed. 
4.Seek out social support. 
5.Connect with others socially. 
6.Maintain a normal routine.
7.Give back to others.
8.Use guided meditation. Guided meditation is a great way to distract yourself from the stress of day-to-day life.
9.Practice deep breathing. 
10.Maintain physical exercise and good nutrition.
