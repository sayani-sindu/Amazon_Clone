BLOOD PRESSURE

Blood pressure is a measure of the force that your heart uses to pump blood around your body.

CAUSES OF BLOOD PRESSURE: 

1.Overweight.
2.Eat too much salt and do not eat enough fruit and vegetables.
3.Do not do enough exercise.
4.Ddrink too much alcohol or coffee (or other caffeine-based drinks)
5.Smoking habits.
6.Do not get much sleep or have disturbed sleep.
7.Age over 65.
8.Not getting enough regular physical activity.

TYPES: 

Blood pressure readings are made up of two values:
1.Systolic blood pressure is the pressure when the heart beats â€“ while the heart muscle is contracting (squeezing) and pumping oxygen-rich blood into the blood vessels.
2.Diastolic blood pressure is the pressure on the blood vessels when the heart muscle relaxes.
3.Based on these two types there are hypertension and hypotension
4.Hypertension: High blood pressure, also called hypertension, is blood pressure that is higher than normal. 
5.Hypotension: Abnormally low blood pressure.

SYMPTOMS: 

1.Headaches.
2.Shortness of breath.
3.Nosebleeds.
4.Moderate or severe headaches, anxiety, shortness of breath, nosebleeds, palpitations, or feeling of pulsations in the neck are some signs of high blood pressure.
5.Symptoms of low blood pressure:
             	lightheadedness or dizziness.
                	feeling sick.
		   	blurred vision.
			generally feeling weak.
			confusion.
			fainting.

PREVENTION: 

1. High blood pressure can often be prevented or reduced by eating healthily, maintaining a healthy weight, taking regular exercise, drinking alcohol in moderation and not smoking.
2. Not doing the below mentioned (hypertension):
			Being overweight or obese.
			Too much salt (sodium) in your diet.
			Too little potassium in your diet.
			Not being physically active.
			Drinking too much alcohol.
3.Prevention of Hypotension:
			Drink more water, less alcohol. Alcohol is dehydrating and can lower blood pressure, even if drinking in moderation.
			Pay attention to body positions. Gently move from lying flat or squatting to a standing position.
			Eat small, low-carb meals. 
			Exercise regularly.
DIET:

The Items that will help you are:

1.Diet for Hypertension:
			Oatmeal. Oatmeal is high in fiber and low in sodium.
			Eggs. Eggs are high in protein and one of the more popular breakfast options.
			Yogurt and Berries.
			Whole Grain Bread, Bagels and English Muffins.
			Unsalted Nuts.
			Dark Chocolate. 
			Fruits and Vegetables. 
			Fruit Smoothies.

			Salty foods can increase blood pressure.
